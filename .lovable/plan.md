

# AVONTAÉ - One-Page Luxury Baby Care Website

## Brand Philosophy
A care-led institution for premature and newborn babies (0-12 months). The website feels like "a quiet care institution, not a fashion store." Calm before commerce.

---

## Site Architecture: One-Page Guided Experience

### Global Elements

**Navigation (Hidden → Revealed)**
- Menu is hidden on initial page load
- Appears elegantly after user begins scrolling
- 5 minimal navigation items: Begin, The Standard, Care, Journal, Contact
- Clicking any item smooth-scrolls to that section
- Logo in header (using your Avontaé logos)
- Cart icon (minimal, no count badge)

**Footer**
- Simple, calm footer with:
  - Logo
  - Contact information
  - Legal links (Privacy, Terms)
  - No promotional language

---

## Section 1: BEGIN (Hero & Products)

**Purpose**: Establish authority and trust before showing products

**Content Flow**:
1. **Hero** - Full viewport with brand statement: "Care before commerce" aesthetic
2. **Brand Philosophy** - Short, calm introduction to Avontaé's purpose
3. **Stage-Based Care Preview** - Subtle introduction to Premature / Newborn / 0-12 months
4. **Product Reveal** - 3-4 featured products appear after context is established
5. **Stage Collections** - Visual cards for Premature, Newborn, Gift Sets that lead to more products

**Design**:
- Massive white space
- Slow reveal animations
- Editorial photography placeholders
- No "Shop Now" buttons dominating the view

---

## Section 2: THE STANDARD

**Purpose**: Build trust through transparency - replaces typical "About" page

**Content**:
1. **Materials Discipline** - Explanation of fabric selection philosophy
2. **Construction Logic** - How garments are made with care
3. **Design Restraint** - Why less is more
4. **Care Philosophy** - Commitment to safety and sensitivity

**Design**:
- Editorial layout with generous spacing
- Typography-focused with minimal imagery
- Clean, structured content blocks

---

## Section 3: CARE

**Purpose**: Reassurance before and after purchase

**Content**:
1. **Handling** - How to handle delicate fabrics
2. **Washing** - Gentle care instructions
3. **Fabric Evolution** - What to expect over time
4. **What to Expect** - Setting proper expectations

**Design**:
- Calm, reassuring tone throughout
- Icon-based guidance
- Expandable details for deeper information

---

## Section 4: JOURNAL

**Purpose**: Cultural depth without marketing feel

**Content Types** (only 3):
1. **Responsibility Notes** - Thoughtful entries on care responsibility
2. **Care Education** - Educational content
3. **Quiet Observations** - Reflective, brand-building content

**Design**:
- Not a blog, not marketing
- Short, thoughtful entries
- Minimal, editorial cards
- Dates but no aggressive sharing buttons

---

## Section 5: CONTACT

**Purpose**: Soft conversion - begin a conversation

**Content**:
1. Short reassurance text
2. Simple contact form (Name, Email, Message)
3. Clear, calm language
4. No pressure CTAs

**Design**:
- Minimal form fields
- Lots of breathing room
- Warm, inviting but not pushy

---

## Product & Cart Experience

**Product Cards** (visible in BEGIN section):
- Image-first, large photography
- Product name in elegant typography
- No badges ("Best Seller", "Hot", etc.)
- Price displayed subtly
- No urgency language

**Product Detail View** (Modal or slide-out panel):
1. Large product imagery
2. Product name
3. Care-led description (not selling features)
4. Fabric & safety information
5. Size/age guidance
6. Care instructions
7. Calm "Add to Cart" button

**Cart** (Slide-out panel):
- Clean, minimal layout
- No cross-sell pressure
- Gentle reminder about care
- Clear totals
- Proceed to Checkout

**Checkout** (Modal or dedicated overlay):
- Step 1: Contact & Address
- Step 2: Payment (mock UI)
- No upsells, no distractions
- Calm confirmation

---

## Visual Design System

**Typography**:
- Editorial hierarchy
- Serif for headings (elegant, timeless)
- Clean sans-serif for body
- No playful or childish fonts

**Colors**:
- Neutral palette: Warm whites, soft grays, muted earth tones
- Dark charcoal for text (matching your logo color)
- No bright or aggressive colors

**Spacing**:
- Generous white space everywhere
- Content must breathe
- If unsure: add more space

**Motion**:
- Slow, subtle scroll animations
- Fade-ins on section entry
- No parallax gimmicks
- Motion feels almost unnoticeable

**Imagery**:
- Reference placeholder images from luxury baby/fashion sources
- Editorial, soft lighting
- No stock-photo feel

---

## Responsive Design

**Desktop**: Full editorial experience with generous layouts
**Tablet**: Adapted spacing, maintaining calm feel
**Mobile**: Hamburger menu, single-column layouts, same calm aesthetic

---

## What Will NOT Be Included

- No pop-ups (newsletter, discounts, exit intent)
- No urgency words (Hurry, Sale, Limited)
- No dense product grids
- No trend-driven UI
- No social proof widgets
- No playful baby clichés
